About us

Welcome to SOSEL.

SOSEL (Ships and Offshore Structures Engineering Laboratory) was established in September 2008 when Professor Joonmo CHOUNG joined Inha University as a faculty member in the Department of Naval Architecture and Ocean Engineering.

Since its establishment, SOSEL has developed world-leading technologies in several research areas, including the ultimate strength of ships and offshore structures, the fatigue strength of ships and offshore structures, ice-resistance estimation for icebreaking vessels, and load and stress analysis of offshore wind turbines.

SOSEL developed the first algorithm in the world capable of predicting the ultimate strength of damaged ships, and implemented it in a software package named UMADS (Ultimate Moment Analyses of Damaged Ships). The laboratory also developed FAFOS (Fatigue Analyses of Floating Offshore Structures), which enables fully statistical fatigue assessment, and has established its own in-house platform for the design of catenary mooring systems.

In addition, SOSEL developed HydroQus (Hydrodynamic Subroutines for Abaqus), which provides a world-first methodology for estimating the ice resistance of icebreaking vessels while accounting for hydrodynamic effects. In the field of floating offshore wind turbines, SOSEL has established a framework for fully coupled load-stress analysis by coupling HydroQus with OpenFAST. More recently, SOSEL developed UnityDyn, which enables fully coupled load-stress analysis for the substructures of fixed offshore wind turbines.

Major Research Topics

  • Structural safety and reliability of ships and offshore structures
  • Floating offshore wind turbine support structures and mooring systems
  • Dynamic power cable mechanics, fatigue, and stress assessment
  • Ultimate strength, collapse behavior, and nonlinear finite element analysis

Industrial Collaboration

  • Design verification and safety assessment
  • Development of engineering procedures and testing frameworks
  • Code-based and simulation-based structural evaluation
